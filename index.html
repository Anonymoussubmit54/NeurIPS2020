Audio samples from "Context Understanding with Contextual Embeddings for Multi-Talker Speech Separation and Recognition in A Cocktail Party"

Authors: Anonymous

Abstract: Multi-talker speech separation and recognition are two key tasks in the cocktail party problem, where multiple speakers talk simultaneously and produce overlapped speech. Although various deep learning based methods have been developed to address these problems, there is still a large performance gap between machines and humans. Inspired by humans' hearing mechanism, which allows us to trace and distinguish the interested speaker from the multi-talker mixed speech by understanding the context, this paper explores the usage of contextual information to improve both monaural speech separation and multi-talker recognition. We propose a novel architecture to learn contextual embeddings from the speech mixture directly and then assist the context understanding for the machines. Specifically, an embedding predictor is learned from a encoder-decoder based single-talker speech recognition teacher model. Then, we explore several approaches to integrate the context embeddings into speech separation and multi-talker speech recognition systems. Furthermore, a novel embedding sampling strategy is designed to improve the model optimization. We evaluate the proposed architectures on the artificially generated WSJ0-2mix datasets, and the newly proposed method brings a large improvement on both multi-talker speech separation and recognition. Additional analysis on the contextual embeddings also demonstrates that these embeddings directly learned from the multi-talker mixed audio can well capture the context information for each speaker, and they can be well segregated on the contextual space which is very useful for the multi-talker speech separation and recognition.
